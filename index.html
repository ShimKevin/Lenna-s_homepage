About Me

My Background

I am a teacher by profession, a graduate from Kenyatta University with a Bachelor's degree in Special Needs. I specialized in intellectual disability with English and Literature as my teaching subjects.

With experience, I have undergone training on different international curricula, including Pearson Edexcel and Cambridge curriculum. I also have experience teaching English as a second language to individuals who are not native speakers of English.

My Experience

I have quality experience in working with learners having needs like autism, intellectual disabilities and learning disabilities. My goal is to help learners with special needs achieve their full potential and become independent individuals.

Helping learners discover and develop their potentialities gives me an opportunity to grow in all aspects.

My Mission

My mission is to create inclusive learning environments where every student feels valued and supported. I believe that with the right approach and dedication, every learner can achieve their full potential.

My Hobbies & Interests

Reading

I'm an avid reader with a particular interest in educational psychology, inclusive teaching methodologies, and literature that explores diverse perspectives. Reading helps me stay current with educational research and find inspiration for my teaching practice.

Exploring Nature

Spending time in nature is my way of recharging. I enjoy hiking, bird watching, and botanical gardens. These experiences often inspire activities I incorporate into my teaching to help students connect with the natural world.

Cooking

Cooking is both a creative outlet and a practical life skill that I enjoy sharing with my students. I experiment with recipes from different cultures and adapt cooking activities to make them accessible for learners with various abilities.

Listening to Music

Music is an important part of my life and my teaching approach. I enjoy diverse genres from classical to world music. I incorporate music therapy techniques in my work with special needs students to enhance learning and emotional expression.
